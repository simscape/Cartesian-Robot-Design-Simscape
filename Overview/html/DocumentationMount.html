Mount

Mount block models the rigid body structural component in a Cartesian robot that supports a linear stage. You can attach a linear stage with a column or another linear stage using a mount. These figures show a mount with Plate mount, L-shaped mount and Angle mount configurations respectively.

Ports

B - Base Frame

Body frame located at the centroid of the bottom surface of the mount. Use this frame to connect a mount to other components, like columns or linear stages.

F - Follower Frame

Body frame located at the centroid of the top or side surface of the mount. Use this frame to connect a mount to other components, like linear stages.

Parameters

Configuration

Mount type

The configuration of the mount, specified as Plate mount, L-shaped mount and Angle mount. These figures show a mount with Plate mount, L-shaped mount and Angle mount configurations respectively.

Mount angle

Angle of the side surface of the mount relative to the bottom surface. To enable this parameter, specify the Mount type as Angle mount.

Rotation angle about mount base

Angle of rotation of the mount about perpendicular to the bottom surface. To enable this parameter, specify the Mount type as L-shaped mount or Angle mount.

Inertia

Inertia type

Inertia type, specified as Custom or Calculate from Geometry. Select Custom to model a distributed mass with the specified moments and products of inertia. Select Calculate from Geometry to enable the block to automatically calculate the rotational inertia properties from the solid geometry and specified mass density of the mount.

Density

Material density of the mount, specified as a positive scalar. The unit is kg/m^3.

Mass

Total mass of the mount, specified as a positive scalar. The unit is kg.

Center of mass

[x y z] coordinates of the center of mass of the mount relative to the body frame located at the Base Frame of mount.

Moments of inertia

Three-element vector with the [Ixx Iyy Izz] moments of inertia specified relative to a frame with origin at the center of mass and axes parallel to the body frame located at the Base Frame of mount. The moments of inertia are the diagonal elements of the inertia tensor

Graphic

Mount color

Color of the mount graphic under direct white light, specified as an [R G B] or [R G B A] vector on a 01 scale. An optional fourth element (A) specifies the color opacity on a scale of 01. Omitting the opacity element is equivalent to specifying a value of 1.
