Column

Column block models the vertical structural component in a Cartesian robot that supports the linear stages and mounts. The column is a rigid body that consists of a bottom plate, a pillar, a top plate and four gusset plates. This figure shows a column.

Ports

B - Base Frame

Body frame located at the centroid of the bottom surface of the bottom plate of the column. Use this frame to connect a column to the world frame.

F - Follower Frame

Body frame located at the centroid of the top surface of the top plate of the column. Use this frame to connect a column to other components, like mount or linear stages.

Inertia

Inertia type

Inertia type, specified as Custom or Calculate from Geometry. Select Custom to model a distributed mass with the specified moments and products of inertia. Select Calculate from Geometry to enable the block to automatically calculate the rotational inertia properties from the solid geometry and specified mass density of the column.

Density

Material density of the column, specified as a positive scalar. The unit is kg/m^3.

Mass

Total mass of the column, specified as a positive scalar. The unit is kg. Mass is distributed to the pillar only and the mass of the bottom plate, top plate and gusset plates is assumed to be zero.

Center of mass

[x y z] coordinates of the center of mass of the column relative to the body frame located at the Base Frame of column.

Moments of inertia

Three-element vector with the [Ixx Iyy Izz] moments of inertia specified relative to a frame with origin at the center of mass and axes parallel to the body frame located at the Base Frame of column. The moments of inertia are the diagonal elements of the inertia tensor

Graphic

Column color

Color of the column pillar graphic under direct white light, specified as an [R G B] or [R G B A] vector on a 01 scale. An optional fourth element (A) specifies the color opacity on a scale of 01. Omitting the opacity element is equivalent to specifying a value of 1.
